3. Caveats

  • The "same-weekday 4-week avg" uses 4 prior Sundays, which may partially include prior Labor Day period weeks, slightly inflating the baseline. The +29.3% lift could be understated vs. a truly "normal" Sunday comparison.
  • Light rain can sometimes suppress walk-in traffic for stores without covered parking, but given the strong sales, any weather drag was clearly more than compensated for by the holiday demand.
  • One store outperforming its tier by 12% suggests local/store-specific factors (e.g., local promotions, inventory events, management initiatives, or a nearby event) are likely at play beyond just the holiday tailwind.
  • Without store capacity/constraint data, we can't tell if $270.80 is at the ceiling or could have been even higher.

Bottom-line assessment: The holiday calendar context explains a broad lift, but store h235's #1 rank overall and +12% over its own tier point to store-specific outperformance on top of the holiday effect. The weather was neutral.
