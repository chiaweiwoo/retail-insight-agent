### RCA Report: Store l185 — 2024-04-13

---

## 1. Trigger

Store l185 saw sales of **$49.40** on Saturday, 2024-04-13. This triggered a **lift signal** at **+54.3%** vs. trailing 7-day average ($32.03) and **+38.4%** vs. the prior day ($35.70). The day was the **highest sales in the trailing 11-day window**.

Sales also exceeded the prior Saturday (Apr 6, Qingming period) by **+19%** ($41.50 → $49.40).

---

## 2. Likely Drivers

1. **Promotional effectiveness (primary driver)**  
   - Promotional sales share jumped to **48.4%** (vs. 38.1% on Friday and 34.5% on prior Saturday), despite a flat promotional product rate (36.6%).  
   - Discount depth and breadth were *not* increased — discounts were slightly shallower and covered fewer products. The sales uplift is thus tied to **promotional mix / customer response**, not broader price cuts.

2. **Store-specific outperformance (context driver)**  
   - Store l185 ranked **#1 out of 5 L-tier stores**, beating the tier average ($40.06) by **+23%**. This is not explained by a broad tier-wide lift.  
   - Weather (mild, light rain) and calendar (Saturday weekend) were favorable but not extreme enough to explain the magnitude.

3. **Demand spike likely overwhelming inventory (contributing factor)**  
   - Sales hit $49.40 despite **68.3% of tracked products hitting a stockout** and average stockout hours of **5.85 hours**. Peak hourly stockout rate was **65.9%**.  
   - The sales level *despite* heavy stockouts suggests **true demand was even higher** than observed sales. Stockouts were likely a *consequence* of strong demand, not the root cause of the sales move.

## 4. Caveats

- **No same-weekday 4-week baseline is available** for any analyst. We cannot compare this Saturday to a *typical* Saturday. The prior Saturday (Apr 6) was part of Qingming holiday, which may have a different demand profile.
- **Stockout data is product-level aggregate** — we don't know which departments or top-selling SKUs were affected. A few high-velocity items being out could drive most of the impact.
- **Causality between stockouts and sales is ambiguous**. The co-occurrence (high sales + high stockouts) could mean either:
  - Demand caused stockouts (inventory couldn't keep up), or
  - Pre-existing stockouts suppressed what *could have been* even higher sales.
- **The lift is recent-acceleration dominated** (3-day ramp from $29.18→$35.70→$49.40) rather than a sustained broad trend shift.
- **L-tier has only 5 stores** — rank #1 is a small-sample distinction.

---

## 5. Suggested Next Checks

1. **Hourly sales x hourly stockout patterns** — Did stockouts peak *after* the sales spike (demand-driven) or *before* (supply-driven)?  
2. **Top-10 selling SKUs** — What were their stockout windows? Did high-velocity items drive the sales or were they missing?  
3. **Promotional item detail** — Which specific promoted SKUs captured the 48.4% sales share? Were they seasonal, weekend-relevant, or holiday-residual?  
4. **Delivery/replenishment logs** — Did store receive a delivery on Apr 13? When was it shelved? The low full stockout rate (2.4%) suggests inventory existed but wasn't on the floor.  
5. **Obtain same-weekday 4-week baseline** for Saturday comparisons, if available from a longer data window.  
6. **Peer comparison within L-tier** — Did other L-tier stores see similar stockout levels on Apr 13, even without the sales spike?
