## 2. Findings

### Sales Movement

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| **Current sales (04-13)** | **$49.40** |
| Prior day (Fri 04-12) | $35.70 |

Sales on 04-13 **spiked sharply** -- up **+38%** vs the prior day, **+54%** vs the trailing 7-day average, and **+19%** vs the most recent comparable Saturday (04-06).

### Pricing / Discounting Assessment

| Metric | 04-13 | 04-12 | 04-06 (last Sat) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Avg discount (1 = full price) | **0.9209** | 0.9182 | 0.9405 |
| Discounted product rate | **46.3%** | 53.7% | 39.0% |
| Deep discount rate | 0.0% | 0.0% | 0.0% |

- The **average depth of discount was nearly identical** to the prior day (0.921 vs 0.918) and actually *less aggressive* than the prior Saturday (0.921 vs 0.941).  
- However, the **share of products receiving any discount shrank** from 53.7% (Fri) to 46.3% (Sat). This is *counterintuitive* to a discount-driven sales jump -- fewer products were on discount.  
- No deep discounting occurred on any of the compared days.

**Conclusion on pricing:** Price reductions were **not the primary driver** of the sales increase. In fact, pricing was slightly less concessional (higher avg discount, lower penetration) than the day prior.

### Promotional Activity Assessment

| Metric | 04-13 | 04-12 | 04-06 (last Sat) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Activity product rate | **36.6%** | 36.6% | 34.2% |
| Activity sales share | **48.4%** | 38.1% | 34.5% |

- The **promotional product rate held steady** at 36.6% -- same as Friday, slightly above the prior Saturday.  
- Critically, **activity's share of sales jumped** to **48.4%** from 38.1% on Friday and 34.5% on the prior Saturday. This is a +10.3pp rise vs the prior day and a +13.9pp rise vs the comparable Saturday.

**Conclusion on promotions:** Promotional products contributed **nearly half of all sales**, a materially higher share than recent days. The mix shifted strongly toward promoted items, even though the raw number of promoted SKUs didn't change. This indicates either (a) better placement/visibility of promotional items, (b) stronger customer response to the specific promotions running, or (c) promotional items becoming more relevant to weekend shopping baskets.

### Summary

| Factor | Contribution to sales move? |
|---|---|
| Discount depth (avg discount rate) | Minimal / neutral |
| Discount breadth (% of items discounted) | Negative (declined) |
| Promotional activity breadth (% of items on promo) | Neutral (flat) |

The $49.40 sales result was **primarily driven by promotional activity effectiveness**, not by broader or deeper discounting. The promotional items that were offered resonated strongly with customers, capturing 48.4% of total revenue despite only representing 36.6% of the SKU base.

## 3. Caveats

- **Same-weekday 4-week average** was unavailable, limiting our ability to benchmark against a normal Saturday pattern. The prior Saturday (04-06) fell during the Qingming holiday period, which may suppress comparability (though Qingming sales were still strong at $41.50).
- **No deep discounting** was observed, meaning the analysis concerns moderate, regular promotional mechanics -- not clearance or loss-leader activity.
- The memo assesses *correlation*, not strict causation. Other factors (store traffic, weather, local events, inventory changes, competitive dynamics) are not captured in the available data but could also explain the sales uplift.
- The **same activity product rate** (36.6%) on both 04-12 and 04-13 suggests the promotion set carried over. The jump in activity sales share could reflect a weekend day-part effect (e.g., weekend shoppers gravitate more toward promoted categories) rather than a deliberate change in promotional strategy.
