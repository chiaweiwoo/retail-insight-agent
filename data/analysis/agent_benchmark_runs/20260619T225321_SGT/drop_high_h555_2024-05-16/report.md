# RCA Report: Store h555 — 2024-05-16

## 1. Trigger

Store h555 fired a **drop signal** on Thursday, 2024-05-16 based on a trailing 7-day average decline of **-24.9%** (threshold: ≤ -20%). Current day sales: **$131.91**.

## 2. Likely Drivers

The sales decline appears driven by a **combination of inventory availability problems and a store-specific local demand issue**, with weather as a minor amplifier. The primary driver cannot be definitively isolated but the evidence points toward:

1. **Elevated stockout rates (likely primary contributor)** — stockout metrics are high enough to materially depress sales
2. **Store-specific local factor** (rather than a broad event) — peers in the same tier with identical weather averaged $161 vs. h555's $132
3. **Rain as a modest headwind** — 1.89mm precipitation likely suppressed foot traffic but does not explain the full gap vs. peers

Pricing/promotional activity is **unlikely to be a driver** — discounting was present (48% of products discounted) but shallow (~7.6% average discount), which is normal-range activity insufficient to counteract other factors.

### Disambiguating the Drivers
The two specialist memos (inventory and peer/weather) offer complementary signals:
- Inventory analyst: "Stockouts are **likely a contributing factor**" — high enough rates to plausibly cause meaningful sales hit
- Context analyst: "The issue is **store-specific or local**" — peers under same conditions fared better

These are **not contradictory**: a store-specific inventory failure (e.g., missed replenishment, poor stock positioning) would make h555 underperform its peers even under identical weather conditions. The stockouts could be the *mechanism* through which a local operational issue depresses sales.

## 4. Caveats

- **Correlation vs. causation is unresolved**: High stockout metrics and low sales co-occur, but without item-level substitution data or foot-traffic counts, we cannot prove stockouts *caused* the decline vs. being a symptom of low demand
- **No product-category breakdown**: Stockouts may be concentrated in high-velocity categories (amplifying impact) or slow movers (diminishing impact)
- **No replenishment timing data**: Unclear whether stockouts are a morning replenishment gap or chronic mid-week inventory position
- **No competitor or local disruption data**: A nearby event, roadwork, or competitor promotion could independently depress traffic regardless of inventory health
- **The same-weekday 4-week average ($151) includes Labor Day period** (May 2 Thursday at $126 — a suppressed baseline), which may understate the severity of the current $132 print
- **No staffing data**: Understaffing could simultaneously cause poor shelf replenishment and poor customer service, compounding the sales impact
- **Pricing data is aggregate**: No SKU-level granularity to see if high-velocity items were missed by promotions

## 5. Suggested Next Checks

1. **Item-level stockout review**: Identify the severely stocked-out products (17.7% severe stockout rate). Are they high-velocity or high-margin items? This would confirm or refute whether stockouts are the primary mechanism.
2. **Replenishment audit**: Check delivery schedules and shelf-stocking timing for the week of May 13-16. Did a missed or delayed delivery occur? Stockouts increasing through the week would support a replenishment failure hypothesis.
3. **Foot traffic data**: If available, compare traffic counts vs. peers for May 16. Flat traffic + lower conversion = stockout problem; lower traffic = demand-side issue.
4. **Peer deep-dive**: Identify which stores in tier h performed well on May 16 and check if they share any pattern (e.g., different staffing, promotions, or inventory levels) that h555 lacks.
5. **Local disruption scan**: Check for road closures, construction, or competitor openings near store h555 that could explain the store-specific underperformance vs. peers.
6. **Category-level pricing review**: Confirm that the 48% discount coverage and 7.6% average discount are indeed normal-range for this store and date (compare to prior Thursdays' discount metrics).
