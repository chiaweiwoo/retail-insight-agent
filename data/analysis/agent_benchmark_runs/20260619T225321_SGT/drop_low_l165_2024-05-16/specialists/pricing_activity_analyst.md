## Memo: Pricing & Promotional Activity Assessment

**Store:** l165  
**Date:** 2024-05-16 (Thursday, normal weekday)  
**Analyst:** Pricing & Activity Specialist

---

### 1. Scope

Assessment of whether pricing or promotional activity contributed to the sales movement at store l165 on 2024-05-16. Current sales: **$25.38**. Key baselines: trailing 7-day avg = $37.17, same-weekday 4-week avg = $28.05.

---

### 2. Findings

**Sales are materially below recent trend.** Current sales ($25.38) are:
- **-24.3%** vs trailing 7-day average ($37.17)
- **-9.5%** vs same-weekday 4-week average ($28.05)
- **-24.2%** vs yesterday ($33.50)

**Discounting was modest.** The average discount factor was **0.9218** (i.e. ~7.8% average discount), with **47.7% of products** receiving some discount. Notably, **no products** were on deep discount (0.0% deep discount rate). This is relatively restrained — the store was not employing heavy price cuts to drive volume.

**Promotional activity was moderate.** About **40.9% of products** were on some form of promotional activity, contributing **39.4% of sales**. This is a meaningful but not dominant share — roughly two-fifths of revenue came from promoted items.

**Assessment:** Pricing/promotion is **unlikely to be the primary driver** of today's sales decline, but may have contributed to the magnitude. The discount posture is restrained (no deep discounts, below ~8% average discount), which means pricing was not aggressive enough to stimulate a sales recovery. However, the sales level ($25.38) is also noticeably below the same-weekday baseline ($28.05), suggesting something **other than pricing** is dragging sales lower — possibly foot traffic, out-of-stocks, or other operational issues.

---

### 3. Caveats

- No deep discounting was in effect, so there is no evidence of promotional overhang or margin-destructive behavior.
- The store may be comping against the Labor Day period earlier in May (May 2–5) which inflated the trailing 7-day average; the same-weekday baseline is a cleaner comparison and suggests a smaller gap (~$2.67).
- Activity data does not reveal *type* of promotion (BOGO, TPR, feature, display); different mechanics could affect customer response differently.
- Store-level sales only — no category or item-level detail to identify if a specific category drove the decline.
