## 2. Likely Drivers

1.  **Weekend + Organic Demand Momentum:** Saturday naturally draws higher sales. This Saturday ($49.40) outperformed even the previous Qingming-holiday Saturday ($41.50). The sales shape shows a clear upward ramp into the weekend: $29.18 (Thu) → $35.70 (Fri) → $49.40 (Sat).
2.  **Strong Promotional Uptake:** The share of revenue from promoted items jumped to **48.4%** (vs. 38.1% on Friday and 33.2% on Thursday), while the number of promoted SKUs held steady (36.6%). Customers chose promoted items at a much higher rate.
3.  **Constrained by Pre-existing Low Inventory (Capping Effect):** The sales lift occurred *despite* severe availability issues. Over two-thirds of products (68.3%) experienced a stockout event, with a severe stockout rate of 39.0%. Analysis suggests this was likely a **chronic low-inventory state** rather than a pure demand surge, meaning sales *would likely have been higher* ($55-65+) if inventory had been adequate.
4.  **Moderate Baseline Amplification:** The trailing-7d average ($32.03) includes several low weekday sales from the post-Qingming period (e.g., $27.8, $29.2), which mechanically inflates the percent change when compared to the $49.40 Saturday.

**Pricing was not a driver.** The average discount was stable (approx. 8% off list), with no deep discounting or fire-sale behavior.

**Weather was neutral.** Light rain (2.18 mm) at 18.4°C has no material directional impact.

### Disagreements & Ambiguity

- **Stockout Cause:** The inventory analyst notes a tension between high sales and high stockouts. The evidence suggests this was a **pre-existing inventory issue** (low replenishment before the weekend) rather than a pure demand surge, as prior weekday sales were modest and not indicative of inventory depletion. A pure demand shock would have shown a sharper intraday sales spike and collapse.
- **Promotion Interpretation:** The jump in promotional sales share (48.4%) could mean the promotion itself drove demand, or that a broader external demand surge (e.g., a local event) happened to overlap with the promoted set. The context analyst found no obvious event, but this cannot be ruled out.

## 4. Caveats

- **No 4-week same-weekday baseline** is available. A true "normal Saturday" benchmark would provide stronger context for the $49.40 figure.
- **No product-level granularity.** Inventory and pricing data are aggregate. A few high-value, in-stock, promoted SKUs could be driving the entire sales figure while most shelves are empty.
- **No intraday timing.** We cannot determine if sales were front-loaded (early sell-out) or spread across the day. The high hourly peak stockout (65.9%) suggests poor availability throughout.
- **No replenishment data.** It is unknown if a delivery was scheduled or missed for 04-13 or the prior days.
- **"Activity" definition** for promotional items is unspecified; it may capture temporary price reductions, featured items, or other in-store mechanics.
