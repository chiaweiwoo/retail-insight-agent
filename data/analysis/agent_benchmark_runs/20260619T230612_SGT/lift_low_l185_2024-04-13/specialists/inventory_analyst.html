1. Scope

Store: l185 | Date: 2024-04-13 (Saturday, weekend) | Total Sales: $49.40

Assessment covers stockout severity, availability pressure, and whether inventory constraints likely contributed to or constrained the observed sales move.

2. Findings

Sales Context

  • Current sales ($49.40) are +54.2% above the trailing 7-day average ($32.03) and +38.4% above the prior day ($35.70 on Friday).
  • The store is coming off a string of normal weekday sales in the $27–$30 range before a Friday uptick and a Saturday jump.
  • No same-weekday 4-week average is available for comparison, but the lift relative to recent history is clearly positive and above typical weekend levels (the prior Saturday, 2024-04-06, was $41.50 during the Qingming period).

Stockout & Availability Pressure — Notably High

  • Avg stockout hours per product: ~5.85 hours — very high for a single selling day.
  • Stockout product rate: 68.3% — more than two-thirds of products experienced a stockout event during the day.
  • Severe stockout rate: 39.0% — nearly 4 in 10 products had prolonged or severe unavailability.
  • Full stockout rate: 2.4% — a small fraction were completely out all day.
  • Hourly stockout peak: 65.9% — meaning at peak hours, two out of three products were unavailable.

Cause vs. Consequence Ambiguity

  • Sales are UP, but availability is POOR. This is a critical tension. Normally, high stockouts suppress sales. The fact that sales are elevated despite severe availability gaps suggests either: 1. Demand surged first (demand shock) — customers bought heavily early, depleting inventory and causing stockouts later in the day. In this scenario, stockouts are a consequence of strong sales, not a cause. 2. Inventory was already thin — low on-hand stock meant that even moderate demand caused rapid sell-through and prolonged out-of-stock windows. In this scenario, inventory issues constrained what could have been even higher sales. 3. The available products that remained in stock were high-value/high-volume — skewing the sales mix upward despite many SKUs being unavailable.

  • Key indicator: Average stockout hours of ~5.85 and a peak hourly rate of 65.9% are extreme. If this were purely demand-driven, we'd expect a sharper intraday pattern (early sell-out, then flat). The sustained high stockout rate across the day suggests chronic low availability rather than a brief demand spike.

Inventory Assessment

  • Likely inventory issue: The data points more toward chronic/pre-existing low inventory than a pure demand surge, because:
  • Prior days' sales (Wed $27.8, Thu $29.2) were modest — not enough to deplete shelves headed into the weekend.
  • The Friday uptick ($35.7) was moderate.

  • Yet by Saturday, 68% of products hit stockout and 39% were severe — this suggests inventory was not adequately replenished ahead of the weekend.

  • Sales likely constrained: Given the high severe-stockout rate, it is plausible that sales would have been even higher (potentially $55–65+) if product availability had been normal. The demand signal (weekend + momentum from Fri) was strong, but fulfillment was limited by empty shelves.

3. Caveats

  • No product-level granularity — we cannot confirm whether the products that remained available were the higher-price-point items driving the $49.40 figure, which would inflate the sales total while masking poor availability.
  • No intraday sales timing — we cannot see if sales were front-loaded (early sell-out pattern) or spread across the day. Hourly stockout peak of 65.9% suggests poor availability throughout.
  • No replenishment data — we don't know if a delivery was scheduled or missed for 04-13 or the preceding days.
  • Same-weekday 4-week average is null, limiting our baseline for a "normal" Saturday performance.
  • The prior Saturday (04-06, $41.50) fell during the Qingming holiday period, which may have altered demand patterns and makes it an imperfect comparison.

Bottom line: Sales are up strongly, but this appears to be despite — rather than because of — very poor in-stock position. Inventory issues likely capped upside rather than caused the positive sales move. The stockout pattern is severe enough to warrant a deeper SKU-level investigation and a review of weekend replenishment planning.
