1. Scope

This memo assesses the pricing and promotional activity contribution to the $146.40 sales recorded at store m041 on 2024-05-12 (Sunday), relative to recent history and comparable windows.

2. Findings

Sales Performance - Current sales ($146.40) are +64.5% vs. the trailing 7-day average ($82.56) and +43.0% vs. the same-weekday 4-week average ($102.37). - This is the highest single-day total in the 14-day lookback period by a wide margin — the next highest was $116.48 on the Labor Day period Sunday (2024-05-05). - The prior day (Saturday 2024-05-11) was $89.00, a normal weekend level. The jump to $146.40 is abrupt and significant.

Pricing / Discounting - Average discount factor is 0.8997 (~10% off on average), with 60.9% of products being discounted. No deep discounts (0.0% deep discount rate). - Discount metrics are notable but not extreme — the average 10% off with ~61% penetration suggests a broad, modest discount program rather than deep clearance activity.

Promotional Activity - 46.4% of products are on some form of promotional activity. - Promotional items contributed 39.4% of total sales — a meaningful share but not dominant (~60% of sales came from non-promoted items). - This implies the promotional activity is widespread in product count but only moderately concentrated in dollar terms, suggesting the uplift is coming from both promoted and non-promoted items.

Assessment of Pricing/Promo Contribution - The discount levels are consistent but moderate — unlikely alone to explain a 64%+ sales spike. - The promo/activity metrics are elevated but again not extreme enough to single-handedly drive nearly 1.5x the 4-week Sunday average. - Mother's Day falls on the second Sunday of May (May 12, 2024). This is the most plausible non-pricing driver: a seasonal gift-giving event. The promotional activity (46% product rate) likely reflects seasonal/feature displays for Mother's Day gifting, and the broad-based sales lift across both promoted and non-promoted items supports a holiday-driven demand surge more than a pure price promotion effect.

Conclusion: Pricing and promotional activity contributed to the sales move indirectly — the discounting and promo mix was consistent with a seasonal event-window strategy (Mother's Day). However, the magnitude of the sales spike is primarily attributable to holiday-driven demand rather than aggressive pricing changes. The discount depth (avg ~10%) is not deep enough to be the primary cause.

3. Caveats

  • Mother's Day attribution is inferred from calendar pattern (2nd Sunday of May) and the holiday context labels in the history data showing "weekend" rather than a named holiday. This should be validated against the retailer's event calendar.
  • No deep discounting was observed, but the avg_discount is a store-wide average — category-level variation could hide important sub-trends.
  • The promotional activity metrics show a moderately high product rate (~46%) but only ~39% of sales — meaning promoted items are under-indexing in share of wallet. This could indicate the promo is on lower-ticket items or that non-promoted high-ticket items (e.g., floral, electronics, jewelry) are driving the dollar volume.
  • A 14-day history window was used; a longer seasonal baseline may provide more stable same-Sunday comparisons.
