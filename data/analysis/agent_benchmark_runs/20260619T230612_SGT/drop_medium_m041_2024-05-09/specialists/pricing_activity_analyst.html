Memo: Pricing & Promotional Activity Assessment – Store m041, 2024-05-09

1. Scope

This memo reviews pricing and promotional activity at store m041 on 2024-05-09 (Thursday, normal weekday). Sales on this date totaled $56.67, representing a notable decline versus recent history.

2. Findings

Sales Trend & Context

  • Current sales ($56.67) are well below the trailing 7-day average ($84.64) and also below the prior day ($75.30).
  • Compared to the same-weekday 4-week average ($67.30), current sales are ~16% lower, suggesting a significant underperformance relative to typical Thursday trading.

Discounting Analysis

  • Average discount factor: 0.9264 (i.e., ~7.4% average discount off full price — moderate).
  • Discounted product rate: 46.4% of products were on some form of discount — a fairly high breadth of discounting.
  • Deep discount rate: 0.0% — no extreme markdowns observed.
  • Interpretation: Discounting was moderately broad but not deep. This level of promotion is typical of standard activity rather than aggressive clearance. Given the sales shortfall, discounting does not appear to have been a strong enough lever or may have been ineffectively targeted.

Promotional Activity

  • Activity product rate: 40.6% of products had some promotional activity (e.g., BOGO, multi-buy, featured displays, etc.).
  • Activity sales share: 39.3% of revenue came from promoted products — roughly proportional to the activity rate, suggesting no strong incremental lift from promotions.
  • Interpretation: Promotional activity was moderate and broad, but it failed to drive above-average sales from promoted items. The share of sales from promoted products aligns closely with their share of assortment, indicating no significant halo effect or uplift.

3. Caveats

  • This analysis reflects correlation, not causation. External factors (foot traffic, weather, local events, stockouts) are not captured in these tools.
  • The discount metric is an average; mix shifts toward lower-priced items could distort the interpretation.
  • Promotional activity data does not specify promotion type (e.g., feature display vs. price cut vs. event), so the quality of promotions is not assessed.
  • Comparison baselines use a limited 14-day history window; seasonality or longer-term trends may affect interpretation.
  • No competitor pricing or local market data is available to benchmark aggressiveness.
