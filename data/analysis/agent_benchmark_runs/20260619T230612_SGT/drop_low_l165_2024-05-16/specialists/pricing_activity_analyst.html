Interpretation:

  • Discount breadth contracted. The share of products under any discount fell from ~52% on May 15 to ~48% on May 16. This is the lowest discount coverage in the recent normal-weekday window (May 14-16).
  • Average discount depth was marginally shallower (7.8% off vs. ~8.2% on the two prior days) -- a very small shift but directionally less promotional.
  • No deep discounting existed on May 16 (0.0%), consistent with the prior two days but less than May 9 (2.3%).
  • Promotional activity share held steady at 40.9% of products (same as May 15), but the activity sales share rose to 39.4% (from 34.9% yesterday). This suggests the promotional products that were active accounted for a larger portion of a smaller overall sales pie.

Verdict on pricing/promotion as a contributor: The sales dip is partially attributable to a slight pullback in promotional intensity (narrower discount coverage, marginally shallower average discount, no deep discounts). However, the magnitude of the sales decline (-24% vs. prior day, -32% vs. 7d avg) is larger than what the minor discount tweaks alone would predict. Other factors (traffic, day-of-week pattern, product mix) are likely significant contributors.

3. Caveats

  • The same-weekday 4wk average ($28.05) indicates Thursday is typically a lower-volume day, so some of the decline is seasonal/weekly pattern.
  • Deep discount rate was already zero on May 14-15, so the removal of deep discounts is not a new change on May 16.
  • Sales data is aggregate -- without item-level or traffic data, we cannot isolate price elasticity or footfall effects.
  • The "activity" metric at 40.9% product rate is identical to May 15, suggesting the promotion calendar didn't change in breadth, only the underlying discount mechanics shifted slightly.
