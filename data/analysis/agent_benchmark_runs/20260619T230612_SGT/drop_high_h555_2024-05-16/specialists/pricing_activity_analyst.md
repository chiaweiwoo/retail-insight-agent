## 1. Scope

This memo analyzes pricing and promotional activity at **store h555** on **2024-05-16** (Thursday, a normal weekday) to assess whether discounting or promotional mechanics contributed to the sales movement observed on that day.

## 2. Findings

### Sales Performance
- **Current sales**: $131.91 — a **drop of -14.6%** vs. the prior day ($154.43 on 2024-05-15).
- **Trailing 7-day average**: $175.63 — current sales are **-24.9% below** that average.
- **Same-weekday 4-week average** (prior Thursdays): $151.03 — current sales are **-12.7% below** that baseline.
- **History trend**: Sales have been declining each day since a local peak on 2024-05-12 ($217.71). The store is on a 4-day downward trajectory.

### Discount Analysis
- **Average discount factor**: 0.9239 (i.e., ~92.4% of full price — a modest ~7.6% average discount).
- **Discounted product rate**: 48.3% — nearly half of the SKUs had some discount applied.
- **Deep discount rate**: 0.0% — no products were on heavy/steep markdown.
- **Interpretation**: Discounts were widespread but shallow. No aggressive price-cutting is evident.

### Promotional Activity
- **Activity product rate**: 38.8% of SKUs were tied to a promotion.
- **Activity sales share**: 38.2% of revenue came from promoted items.
- **Interpretation**: A meaningful portion of the assortment and sales were under promotion, but not dominating.

### Assessment of Pricing/Promotion Contribution
**Pricing** likely did NOT drive the sales decline:
- The average discount (7.6% off) is consistent with routine levels, not a new or aggressive pricing action.
- No deep discounts were deployed.

**Promotional activity** likely did NOT drive the decline either:
- Promotional coverage (38.8% of products, 38.2% of sales) is moderate — it would typically support rather than suppress sales.
- The share of promoted sales is aligned with the product rate, suggesting no concentrated promotional push that could cannibalize non-promoted items.

**Conclusion**: The sales decline appears attributable to **other factors** (e.g., natural post-weekend/weekend demand fade, foot traffic changes, or out-of-stocks) rather than pricing or promotional missteps. The discount and promo mechanics are within normal ranges for this store.

## 3. Caveats

- This analysis relies on store-level aggregate metrics. Item-level or category-level data could reveal sub-groups where pricing or promotion had a different directional impact.
- The baseline comparisons (trailing 7d, same-weekday 4w) do not adjust for holiday shifts — though 2024-05-16 is flagged as a normal weekday post the Labor Day period, some residual holiday effects may linger.
- The average discount factor (0.9239) is an arithmetic mean; mix-shift effects (e.g., more low-price items discounted) could mask underlying pricing behavior.
- No data on competitor pricing, local events, or supply disruptions was available for this assessment.
