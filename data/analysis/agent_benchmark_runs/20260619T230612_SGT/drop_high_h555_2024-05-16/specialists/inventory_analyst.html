2. Findings

Sales Performance - Current sales: $131.91, a -25% decline vs the trailing 7-day average of $175.63. - Compared to the same-weekday 4-week average ($151.03), today is -12.7% below that baseline. - The prior day (Wed 2024-05-15) was $154.43; the prior Thursday (2024-05-09) was $160.00. Stockout / Availability Pressure - Avg stockout hours: 2.96 hours — meaning the average product was unavailable for ~3 hours of the trading day. Interpretation The inventory picture shows clear availability degradation on 2024-05-16. More than a third of the product line saw interruptions, and the average product was missing for roughly 3 hours. Combined with the sales shortfall, this signals that inventory availability issues likely contributed meaningfully to the sales decline. The $43.72 drop versus the trailing weekly average is roughly 25%, which is directionally consistent with the breadth of stockouts observed.

That said, causality is not fully provable from this data alone — demand could have been naturally lower (Thursday vs. the weekend days in the 7-day window were stronger). However, the same-weekday 4-week average ($151.03) also exceeded today's actual by ~$19, and the prior Thursday was $160, so the downward move is real and not just a weekday artifact.

3. Caveats

  • Cause vs. consequence ambiguity: Stockouts could be a symptom of unanticipated demand (if inventory was set correctly but demand spiked) or a cause of lost sales (if inventory was simply insufficient). The data here shows correlation, not causation.
  • No category-level granularity: The aggregate stockout metrics may mask a specific category or top-SKU issue that drives most of the revenue impact.
  • Baseline sales include days with varying stockout conditions: The 7-day average includes days that may themselves have had stockouts, so the true "unconstrained demand" gap could be larger than measured.
  • No promotion/marketing context: If there was a planned promotional event, the stockout rate could reflect demand exceeding supply — a different RCA path than chronic under-stocking.
  • No competitor or external demand signal: A local event, competitor opening, or weather effect could suppress traffic independently of inventory.
